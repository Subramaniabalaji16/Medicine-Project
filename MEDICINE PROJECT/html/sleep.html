Diazepam

Diazepam is a benzodiazepine used to treat anxiety, muscle spasms, and seizures. It works by calming the brain and nerves. It's often prescribed for short-term use due to the risk of dependence and drowsiness.

Alprazolam

Alprazolam, also a benzodiazepine, is used to manage anxiety and panic disorders. It provides rapid relief by enhancing the calming effect of GABA in the brain. Long-term use may lead to dependence, so it's used under close supervision.

Sertraline

Sertraline is an SSRI antidepressant that balances serotonin levels in the brain. It's prescribed for depression, OCD, PTSD, and anxiety. It generally takes a few weeks to show results and is considered safe for long-term use under guidance.

Fluoxetine

Fluoxetine is another SSRI used for depression, panic attacks, and eating disorders. It increases serotonin in the brain, improving mood and energy levels. It's widely prescribed and has a good safety profile with proper medical supervision.
