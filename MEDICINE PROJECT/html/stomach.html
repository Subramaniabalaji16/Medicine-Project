Omeprazole

Omeprazole is a proton pump inhibitor (PPI) used to treat acid reflux, ulcers, and indigestion. It works by reducing the amount of acid your stomach produces, providing relief from heartburn and helping heal damage to the stomach lining.

Ranitidine (Zantac)

Ranitidine, also known as Zantac, is an H2 blocker used to reduce stomach acid. It is used for treating heartburn, gastritis, and peptic ulcers. Although effective, its use has declined due to safety concerns in some formulations.

Pantoprazole

Pantoprazole is a PPI used to treat acid-related stomach issues such as GERD and ulcers. It decreases acid production and is commonly prescribed for long-term treatment of reflux symptoms and esophageal damage.

Domperidone

Domperidone is a medication that helps move food through the stomach and intestines faster. It is often used to relieve bloating, nausea, and vomiting, particularly in cases of delayed gastric emptying or indigestion.

ORS (Oral Rehydration Salts)

ORS is a simple solution of salts and sugars used to treat dehydration caused by diarrhea or vomiting. It helps replenish lost fluids and electrolytes quickly and is a life-saving remedy in many parts of the world.
